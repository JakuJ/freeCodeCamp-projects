Friedrich Nietzsche

Who was Nietzsche?

A German philosopher and cultural critic who published intensively in the 1870s and 1880s. He is famous for uncompromising criticisms of traditional European morality and religion, as well as of conventional philosophical ideas and social and political pieties associated with modernity. Many of these criticisms rely on psychological diagnoses that expose false consciousness infecting peopleâ€™s received ideas. Nietzsche also used his psychological analyses to support original theories about the nature of the self and provocative proposals suggesting new values that he thought would promote cultural renewal and improve social and psychological life by comparison to life under the traditional values he criticized.

Timeline

  • 1844: Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche is born on October 15 in Rocken, a small village near Lutzen
  • 1849: Death of Nietzsche's father on July 30, diagnosed as softening of the brain.
  • 1850: Nietzsche's brother, Ludwig Joseph, dies on January 9. The family relocates to Naumburg in early April.
  • 1856: Nietzsche writes his first philosophical essay 'On the Origin of Evil'.
  • 1858: In October he is accepted into the Pforta School.
  • 1862: Together with a few friends, he founds the literary club 'Germania'.
  • 1864: In October he begins studying theological and classical philology in Bonn.
  • 1865: Leaves Bonn and moves to Leipzig to study philology. In October he discovers Schopenhauer.
  • 1868: Becomes friends with the German composer, Richard Wagner.
  • 1869: Appointed as professor at the University of Basel.
  • 1870: Military service as a medic. He falls ill with dysentery and diphtheria.
  • 1872: Nietzsche's first major work, The Birth of Tragedy is published. It is rejected by scholars.
  • 1876: He makes up his mind to break apart from Wagner.
  • 1879: Resigns from his university teaching and starts his nomadic life.
  • 1881: His first visit to Sils-Maria. Has his great 'inspiration' and writes of eternal recurrence.
  • 1882: Proposes marriage to Lou Salome twice.
  • 1883: Death of Wagner on February 13.
  • 1885: Nietzsche's sister marries Bernhard Forster in May.
  • 1889: Nietzsche breaks down and never recovers from mental illness.
  • 1900: Dies on August 25. Against his wishes, his sister hires a priest to speak at his funeral.
